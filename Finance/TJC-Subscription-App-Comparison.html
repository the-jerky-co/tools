Subscription app shortlist: Seal vs Loop vs Recharge — at 364 subscribers and A$130 AOV, where should TJC land?

A grounded look at pricing, features, customer-portal UX, integrations and out-of-stock handling — with Total Cost of Ownership modelled at TJC's actual scale, in AUD.

Current: Seal Subscriptions
Subscribers: 364
Online AOV: A$130
Trigger: Portal UX, integrations, OOS chain bugs

One-line verdict

Loop Subscriptions is the right upgrade at TJC's current scale. Recharge is overbuilt and meaningfully more expensive per year; Seal is cheap but is the direct cause of the OOS chain-reaction bugs and portal gaps you're already feeling. Loop sits in the middle on price, beats both on customer-portal UX, and has the most flexible out-of-stock logic of the three.

The brief

What's actually breaking right now

Before comparing features, the three specific issues you flagged — mapped to what each app does about them. This is the lens for the rest of the review.

01 · OOS chain bugs

Seal's default behaviour still processes subscription renewals even when a linked product is out of stock, with a warning in admin — which can cascade into broken orders, refunds, or split fulfilment. Inventory skip logic exists but is blunt.

Highest priority

02 · Customer portal / UX

Seal's portal is functional but dated. Loop is the clear category leader on portal UX — passwordless login, mobile-first, fully customisable, one-click skip/swap/pause. Recharge has improved but still feels legacy.

High priority

03 · Scaling & integrations

Seal has basic Klaviyo + GA4 hooks; Loop and Recharge both ship deep Klaviyo, Gorgias, Segment, Attentive and headless-grade APIs. Loop's API is newer but evolving quickly; Recharge's is mature but the admin UX is dated.

Medium priority

The honest read: Loop costs ~22× more than Seal on raw fees. But it processes the same revenue at 1.29% vs Seal's 0.06% — meaning it's still a tiny share of the revenue you're collecting. The break-even point is recovering ~A$7k/yr in extra subscription revenue from better retention, dunning, or fewer broken OOS orders. At 364 subs, that's a 2–3% churn improvement. Loop's published case studies show 25%+ dunning recovery and ~30% reduction in cancellations from their save flows — even at half those rates you're well ahead.

What this means in plain English

Switching from Seal to Loop costs roughly A$7k/yr extra — about A$1.62 per subscription order. Recharge Standard costs ~A$9.5k/yr extra, or A$2.27 per order. Seal is essentially free per order (A$0.08), but it's the source of the chain bugs and the portal gap. The trade isn't "cheap vs expensive" — it's "leak-prone tool you barely pay for vs paid tool that plugs the leaks." On the numbers above, Loop pays for itself the moment it prevents one decent-sized churn cohort or a handful of OOS-driven refunds.

How painful is the switch?

Migration plan & risk

Moving 364 subscribers is a controlled operation — not a big-bang. Both Loop and Recharge offer free, white-glove migrations. Here's the realistic timeline and the risks worth pre-empting.

Week 0 · Pre-migration

Export Seal data, lock in migration window

Export active subscriptions from Seal (customer, SKU, cadence, next-charge date, discount). Most migration teams ask for a read-only CSV or API token. Agree on a cut-over weekend to minimise churn risk.

Week 1 · Build & test

New app installed, portal branded, flows built

Configure Loop/Recharge. Rebuild OOS rules (partial billing thresholds, delay logic), cancellation flows, Klaviyo events. Test migration on a sandbox subscriber set first.

Week 2 · Migrate tokens + go live

Payment tokens + schedules transferred

Loop's process migrates Shopify-stored payment tokens without re-authentication — no customer disruption. Go-live is usually an overnight cut-over. Seal stays running until tokens confirm transferred.

Week 3 · Monitor & reconcile

First renewal cycle audit

Watch the first full renewal cycle. Audit that all subscribers charged correctly, discounts carried over, OOS logic fired as expected. Keep Seal in read-only mode for 30–60 days as a fallback.

Key risks

  • Discount codes not preserved during migration
  • Next-charge-date drift on long-interval subs
  • Klaviyo event schema changes — flows break silently
  • Customers confused by portal URL change — brief heads-up email required

Risk mitigations

  • Insist on test-migration report before go-live
  • Run Seal + Loop in parallel for 30 days
  • Pre-migration subscriber email: "We're improving your portal"
  • Rebuild 2–3 Klaviyo flows (skipped order, OOS delay, failed payment) in week 1
The call

Recommendation

Go with

Loop Subscriptions (Starter plan)

At 364 subs with A$130 AOV, Loop is the sweet spot. You get best-in-class customer portal UX, fixable-by-configuration OOS handling (the specific bug biting you now), native Gorgias + Klaviyo + Attentive integration, and free migration. Roughly A$7k/year more than Seal — which is easily recovered if it prevents even 2–3% of churn or a handful of broken OOS orders that become refunds/support tickets.

Why not Recharge

  • +A$9,500–A$14,000/yr premium over Seal — you're paying for capabilities you won't use yet
  • Admin UX is dated; merchant reviews in 2025 trend negative on innovation
  • Contract tiers create friction if you grow past a threshold mid-year
  • Reconsider at ~2,000 subs or when you add B2B/wholesale subs — Recharge earns its price at that scale

Why not stay on Seal

  • The OOS behaviour is architectural — a config tweak won't fix the chain bugs
  • Portal UX ceiling is low; churn save flows aren't there
  • Cost-optimising against a tool that's leaking revenue is a false economy
  • Seal remains an excellent choice for brands under 100 subs — TJC has outgrown it

What to do this week

  • 1. Book a 30-min Loop demo — explicitly ask them to walk through OOS partial billing and delayed-order flows
  • 2. Request a test migration quote and timeline (typically free, 2 weeks)
  • 3. Pull Seal churn + refund data for the last 90 days — quantify what OOS chain bugs have actually cost you
  • 4. If Loop demo disappoints, get a Recharge Standard quote as fallback — still better than staying on Seal
